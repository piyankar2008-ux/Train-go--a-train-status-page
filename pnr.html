PNR Status Guide

PNR Status Check
 PNR Status tells the latest seat berth status of passenger in Indian Railways i.e. whether passenger has got confirmed reservation, Reservation Against Cancellation (RAC) or the passenger is in waiting list. For waiting list or RAC passengers, PNR status might change to confirmed in future if other passengers on this trip cancel their journey. For passengers who have cancelled their trip, it is shown as Can/Mod. Train PNR status also tells the origin station, boarding point, destination station, travel class, quota and charting status. Once final chart is prepared, the Indian Railway PNR status live freezes and does not change after that.

PNR status - Why is it important?
 It is a record in the Indian Railways database that stores the journey details for a passenger or a group of passengers. The Centre for Railway Information Systems (CRIS) also keeps track of information such as passengers' current PNR status, availability of berths in all classes for the next 60 days, and static inquiries such as Train Between Important Stations, Reservations Rules, Fares, Refund, and Rules, and so on.

The PNR number is typically printed in the upper left corner of printed tickets. The E-Ticket is mentioned at the top in a separate cell. Passengers' personal information, such as name, age, gender, and so on, is saved in the database in conjunction with this reference number. It has columns for storing the booking status and the current status of the ticket.

Because every train has a limited number of seats, it is possible that you will not receive a confirmed reserved ticket. When there is any availability of reserved seats due to cancellation, the current status of such a waitlist (W/L) ticket changes. PNR live status gives all the details in real-time to help you make informed decisions while booking train tickets online.

Enter the 10-digit PNR number found on the top left corner of a train ticket and go to the "PNR Status" button. It will return your train ticket's accurate PNR status as well as waitlist prediction updates.

What does a PNR number explain?
PNR number holds various information related to the train journey.

Passenger Details - Name, Age, Sex, Birth Preference
Ticket Details - Train Number, Date of Journey, Origin/Source Station, Destination Station, Boarding Station, Class, Berth, Quota
Fare, Transaction Type and Payment Details
First 3 digits tell us from which Passenger Reservation System or PRS the ticket has been booked.
First digit depends on the Zone of the Train, w.r.t. starting station of the train.
Zonal Codes - 1 (SCR Secunderabad PRS);
2, 3 (NR, NCR, NWR, NER New Delhi PRS);
4, 5 (SR, SWR, SCR Chennai PRS);
6, 7 (NFR, ECR, ER, ECoR, SER, SECR Calcutta PRS);
8, 9 (CR, WCR, WR Mumbai PRS)
Last seven digits are randomly generated numbers using the Rand() function. These numbers represent no information about the ticket or journey as such. They only provide an unique identity to the PNR number.

When to check PNR status?
It is important to check your PNR status after train ticket booking online. Your PNR status will tell you whether the ticket has been confirmed, on waiting list (WL) or is under reservation against cancellation (RAC). It provides details of the coach and seat number, class of travel, chart status, train name, train number, booking status, current status, journey time and fare apart from the basic information about arrival and departure time of trains.

How many days can we check PNR status? The validity of the PNR expires when the journey is completed. Once the train arrives at the destination station, the PNR becomes meaningless, and no further journeys with the same PNR are possible. However, the PNR number is stored for nine months in the database of Indian Railways (CRIS)

Will my PNR be confirmed?

Because there are only a limited number of seats available on a train, it is not possible to provide a confirmed train ticket online for every booking. When tickets are available, the train ticket booking system displays CNF (Confirmed) and RAC (Reservation Against Cancellation) status, and subsequent bookings display waitlisted status. Only if a CNF/RAC ticket is cancelled will waitlisted tickets be confirmed in order.

Tracking Indian Railway PNR status live is especially important if you have a waitlisted ticket. Other travel information such as coach number, berth number, and berth type are only provided if the booked ticket status is Confirmed (CNF).

Types of PNR Status

  • CNF - 'Confirmed' ticket.
  • RAC - 'Reservation Against Cancellation' (You can board the train).
  • WL - 'Waiting List' (You can only board if it gets confirmed).
  • GNWL - General Waitlist.
  • TQWL - Tatkal Waitlist.
  • PQWL - Pooled Quota Waitlist.
  • RLWL - Remote Location Waitlist.
  • NOSB - No Seat Berth.
  • CAN - Ticket Cancelled.

The 10-digit PNR number can tell you all about your reservation status. This can further enhance your understanding and let you book dorms and meals on trains accordingly.

PNR Status Confirmation Of Waitlist Tickets

  • TQWL - Lower confirmation chances than GNWL
  • RLWL - Less chances of confirmation
  • PQWL - Less chances of confirmation
  • RLGN - Less chances of confirmation under RLWL
  • RQWL - Less chances of confirmation
  • RSWL - Less chances of confirmation

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Where can I find the PNR number on my ticket?
The PNR number is printed on the top left corner of physical tickets and at the top of e-tickets. It's a unique 10-digit number used to check your booking status.
2. Is PNR number allotted to unreserved tickets?
No, PNR numbers are only issued for reserved tickets. Unreserved tickets are not linked to a specific seat or berth, hence no PNR is generated.
3. How is a PNR number generated?
A PNR is generated using a combination of PRS zone code and 7 random digits. It is assigned when a booking is made and is unique for every reservation.
4. How can I check PNR status live?
You can check live PNR status online via platforms like Trainman, IRCTC, or NTES. Just enter your 10-digit PNR number to view the current status and seat confirmation.
5. Do I need to carry any other documents with a valid ticket?
Yes, along with a valid ticket, you must carry a government-issued photo ID such as Aadhaar, PAN, or Voter ID for verification during travel.
6. PNR and Train Charting
PNR status gets finalized once train charting is done. After chart preparation, no further changes in seat status occur, and the final allotment is reflected in the PNR.
7. How are PNR status and train charting related?
Train charting is the process where final seat allocation is done. PNR status becomes fixed once the chart is prepared, usually a few hours before departure.
8. Is there an app to check PNR live status on mobile?
Yes, apps like our site, IRCTC Rail Connect, and NTES allow you to check PNR status, train schedules, and seat availability directly from your smartphone.
9. My booking shows WL but current status is CNF. Is it confirmed?
Yes, CNF means your ticket is confirmed. Though initially waitlisted, it got upgraded due to cancellations or availability during charting.
10. My current status is RAC/20/GN. What does this mean?
RAC means Reservation Against Cancellation. You are allowed to board and will get a seat to sit, but not a full berth until it’s upgraded.
11. Status of my train ticket PNR enquiry is CNF/B1/16/NC. Does that mean my ticket is confirmed or not?
Yes, CNF means your ticket is confirmed. B1 is the coach number, 16 is your seat number, and NC indicates No Choice for berth preference.
12. PNR status check for railway ticket is showing the current status as PQWL/5. Will it confirm or not? What should I do?
PQWL means Pooled Quota Waiting List. It depends on cancellations in the pooled quota. It might get confirmed but is less likely than GNWL.
13. Does checking PNR status let me know the passenger name, age or gender?
No, passenger details like name, age, and gender are not shown publicly for privacy reasons while checking PNR status online.
14. Are these PNR numbers ever reused?
No, each PNR is unique for a journey and is not reused. However, old PNRs are removed from the database after around 9 months.
15. What happens if my railway PNR remains waitlisted after chart preparation?
If your ticket remains waitlisted even after charting, you cannot board the train and the ticket is automatically canceled with refund (for e-ticket).
16. I have lost my train ticket but I remember my PNR number. Can I still travel?
Yes, if you booked online (e-ticket), you can show the ticket on mobile with a valid ID. For paper tickets, you may need to visit the reservation office.
17. I booked a ticket in sleeper (SL) class but my ticket has been upgraded to third ac (3A). Can class get upgraded for any railway PNR?
Yes, Indian Railways may upgrade tickets to higher classes based on availability under the auto-upgrade scheme, at no extra cost.
18. Live PNR status is showing that the train is cancelled. How can a train get cancelled? Will I be getting refund?
Trains can be cancelled due to weather, maintenance, or operational issues. For canceled trains, a full refund is processed automatically if ticket was booked online.
